Online Safety Tips

  • Use Strong Passwords:
    Create complex passwords that include a mix of letters, numbers, and special characters. Avoid using easily guessable information such as birthdays or common words.

  • Put The 4-digit PIN For Security:
    Whenever possible, use two-factor authentication for an added layer of security.

  • Be Cautious with Emails:
    Do not open attachments or click on links from unknown senders. Be wary of phishing emails that ask for personal information or direct you to suspicious websites.

  • Keep Software Up-to-Date:
    Regularly update your operating system, browser, and other software to protect against security vulnerabilities.

  • Use Secure Connections:
    Ensure websites are secure by looking for “https” in the URL and a padlock icon in the address bar. Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions, such as online banking.

  • Protect Personal Information:
    Limit the amount of personal information you share online. Be mindful of the privacy settings on social media platforms and review them regularly.

  • Beware of Scams:
    Be cautious of offers that seem too good to be true. Verify the legitimacy of websites and online stores before making purchases.

  • Regularly Monitor Accounts:
    Check your financial and online accounts regularly for any unauthorized activity. Report any suspicious activity to the appropriate authorities immediately.

  • Use Antivirus Software:
    Install and maintain antivirus software to protect against malware and other threats. Perform regular scans to detect and remove any potential threats.

  • Educate Yourself:
    Stay informed about the latest online security threats and best practices. Participate in online safety courses and follow reputable cybersecurity blogs and websites.
